As urban populations continue to grow, the energy demands of commercial buildings like malls have become a significant concern.

What are we doing?

Smart hardware like adaptive air conditioners, escalators, and lighting systems optimize energy usage based on real-time data. Complementary software platforms raise awareness and encourage behavioral changes, empowering individuals to contribute. This integrated approach of intelligent technology and user engagement drives a holistic shift towards sustainable energy practices across homes, businesses, and communities.

Why are we doing this?

Reducing energy consumption is crucial to combat climate change, conserve natural resources, and ensure a sustainable future. Excessive energy use, predominantly from fossil fuels, contributes to greenhouse gas emissions, environmental degradation, and depletion of finite energy sources. Adopting energy-efficient practices and technologies is vital to mitigate the impact on the planet and secure a livable world for generations to come. As urban populations and the sizes of malls continue to grow, the energy demands of commercial buildings like malls have reached unprecedented levels. Malls are large, complex facilities with significant cooling, lighting, and equipment loads, making them major contributors to a city's overall energy consumption and carbon footprint. So what can we do? Implementing effective energy optimization strategies in malls is crucial for addressing environmental concerns, reducing utility costs, and promoting sustainable urban development. Malls face distinct challenges when it comes to energy management, often due to their size, diversity of tenants, and constant flow of visitors. Factors such as varying operating hours, high occupancy rates, and the need to maintain comfortable temperatures and lighting levels for shoppers can make it difficult to optimize energy usage. Additionally, the integration of diverse systems, from HVAC to escalators, adds complexity to the task of identifying and addressing inefficiencies.

Background Information

Malls are significant energy consumers, accounting for nearly 20% of total commercial building energy usage in the United States. On average, a mid-sized mall can consume over 15 million kWh of electricity annually, equivalent to powering hundreds of homes. This energy-intensive footprint underscores the pressing need to implement sustainable solutions. The escalators in a typical mall consume as much energy as 20 homes per year. Lighting accounts for over 40% of a mall's total energy usage, with parking lots being the single largest energy drain. An average-sized department store inside a mall uses enough energy annually to power 30 single-family homes. Malls with energy-efficient upgrades have reported energy savings of 30-50% without compromising comfort or operations.
